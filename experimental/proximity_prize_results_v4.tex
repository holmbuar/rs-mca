\begin{abstract}
We collect the strongest unconditional Reed--Solomon mutual correlated agreement results currently available in the four principal manuscripts and the theorem-ready part of the \texttt{rs-mca} repository.  Exact affine-line staircases give complete thresholds for four maximal-dimension smooth prime-field rows, an exact $\mathbb F_{17^{32}}$ row, additive binary rows, separating-field rows, and several saturated or post-Johnson examples.  Unconditional corridor theorems give substantially larger safe radii at rates $1/4$, $1/8$, and $1/16$, while universal field-size caps and identity-prefix constructions provide complementary unsafe bounds near capacity.  We also state the exact high-agreement line, curve, and interleaved-list ledger and summarize the strongest unconditional KoalaBear and Mersenne-31 structural packets.  This is a results-only synthesis: proofs and certificate arithmetic are not reproduced, and every assertion is accompanied by a source where the proof or exact replay can be found.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope, conventions, and source hierarchy}

The Proximity Prize asks, for a smooth Reed--Solomon code $C=\RS_{\F}(D,k)$ and a target such as $\eps^*=2^{-128}$, for the largest radius at which the mutual correlated agreement error is at most $\eps^*$ \cite{ProximityPrize,ABF26}.  Here $D$ has size $n$, usually a multiplicative coset of power-of-two order, and the official rates are $k/n\in\{1/2,1/4,1/8,1/16\}$.  This paper is a theorem catalogue, not a replacement for the cited proof papers.

For a nonempty challenge set $\Gamma\subseteq\F$, let $B_{C,\Gamma}^{\MCA}(a)$ be the maximum number of slopes $\gamma\in\Gamma$ for which a point on a received affine line is explained by a degree-$<k$ polynomial on some support of size at least $a$, while the two generators of the received line are not simultaneously explained on that same support.  Put
\[
 e_{\MCA,\Gamma}(C,\delta)
 =\frac{B_{C,\Gamma}^{\MCA}(n-\floor{\delta n})}{\abs\Gamma},
 \qquad B^*=\floor{\eps^*\abs\Gamma}.
\]
If integer radius $r_0$ is the first unsafe radius, the largest safe grid radius is $(r_0-1)/n$, while the supremum of the real safe set is $r_0/n$ and is not attained.

The source hierarchy is as follows.  Exact staircases, shortening bounds, finite rows, saturation statements, and paving compilers are in \cite{ChoShort26,ChoThresholds26}.  Universal field-size caps, smooth and circle transports, and deployed identity-prefix floors are in \cite{ChoCap26}.  The active structural and finite completion architecture is in \cite{ChoGF26}, while its companion reductions and $S{+}A{+}E$ handoff are in \cite{ChoComp26}.  External safe-side bounds are taken from \cite{GKL24,Hab25} through the exact corridor audit \cite{Corridor26}.  Repository-local theorems are cited to immutable notes or packets at commit \texttt{5ecb9ab5}; the coordination log is not used as proof authority.

\section{Exact affine-line staircases}

\begin{theorem}[Support, tangent, and sparsification package]\label{thm:basic}
Let $C=\RS_{\F}(D,k)$, let $a\ge k+1$, put $r=n-a$, and let $\varnothing\ne\Gamma\subseteq\F$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=*]
\item Every bad slope has a witness on a support of size exactly $a$, and one exact support carries at most one bad slope.  Hence
\[
 B_{C,\Gamma}^{\MCA}(a)\le\min\left\{\abs\Gamma,\binom na\right\}.
\]
\item The universal tangent construction gives
\[
 B_{C,\Gamma}^{\MCA}(a)\ge\min\{\abs\Gamma,r+1\}.
\]
\item If $B_{C,\Gamma}^{\CA}(a)$ is the column-far correlated-agreement numerator and $\sigma_{C,\Gamma}(a)$ the sparse mutual numerator with union support at most $r$, then
\[
 B_{C,\Gamma}^{\MCA}(a)=\max\{B_{C,\Gamma}^{\CA}(a),\sigma_{C,\Gamma}(a)\}.
\]
\item If $2r\le n-k$, then $\sigma_{C,\Gamma}(n-r)=\min\{\abs\Gamma,r\}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite{ChoShort26,ChoThresholds26}; the exact support-wise line formulation is also in \cite{LineBridge26}.}

\begin{theorem}[Quadratic staircase]\label{thm:quadratic}
If $0\le r\le n-k-1$ and
\[
 (n-r)^2\ge n(k+r),
\]
then
\[
 \boxed{B_{C,\Gamma}^{\MCA}(n-r)=\min\{\abs\Gamma,r+1\}.}
\]
Equivalently, the formula holds for every $0\le r\le r_{\mathrm{quad}}(n,k)$, where
\[
 r_{\mathrm{quad}}(n,k)=\floor{\frac{3n-\sqrt{n(5n+4k)}}2}.
\]
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite[Quadratic mean-overlap theorem]{ChoShort26} and \cite[Quadratic staircase]{ChoThresholds26}.}

\begin{corollary}[Exact target compiler]\label{cor:target}
Let $B=\floor{\eps\abs\Gamma}$ and assume
\[
 1\le B<\abs\Gamma,
 \qquad B\le n-k-1,
 \qquad (n-B+1)^2\ge n(k+B-1).
\]
Then
\[
 \boxed{\{\delta:e_{\MCA,\Gamma}(C,\delta)\le\eps\}=[0,B/n),}
\]
and the endpoint $B/n$ is unsafe.
\end{corollary}
\source{\cite[Exact target compiler]{ChoShort26,ChoThresholds26}.}

\begin{theorem}[Official-rate staircase almost to half distance]\label{thm:official}
Assume $n=2^e\ge128$, $k=\rho n$, and $\rho\in\{1/2,1/4,1/8,1/16\}$.  Put
\[
 r_\rho^\sharp=
 \begin{cases}
 n/4-3,&\rho=1/2,\\
 3n/8-2,&\rho=1/4,\\
 7n/16-2,&\rho=1/8,\\
 15n/32-2,&\rho=1/16.
 \end{cases}
\]
For every nonempty $\Gamma$ and every $0\le r\le r_\rho^\sharp$,
\[
 \boxed{B_{C,\Gamma}^{\MCA}(n-r)=\min\{\abs\Gamma,r+1\}.}
\]
Whenever $1\le B=\floor{\eps\abs\Gamma}<\abs\Gamma$ and $B\le r_\rho^\sharp+1$, the complete safe set is $[0,B/n)$.
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite{ChoShort26,ChoThresholds26}; the CA input is the two-radius theorem of \cite{BCHKS25}.}

\begin{theorem}[Exact $\mathbb F_{17^{32}}$ smooth threshold]\label{thm:f17}
Let $H\le\mathbb F_{17^{32}}^*$ have order $512$ and let $C=\RS_{\mathbb F_{17^{32}}}(H,256)$.  At target $2^{-128}$,
\[
 B_C^{\MCA}(506)=7,
 \qquad B_C^{\MCA}(507)=6,
 \qquad \floor{17^{32}/2^{128}}=6.
\]
Hence the complete real safe set is
\[
 \boxed{[0,6/512),}
\]
the largest safe grid radius is $5/512$, and $6/512=3/256$ is the unsafe supremal endpoint.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Saturated and post-Johnson finite rows]\label{thm:saturation-finite}
Let $p=(2^{128}-255)2^{128}+1$.  At target $2^{-128}$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=*]
\item if $D\le\F_p^*$ has order $128$ and $k\in\{64,32,16,8\}$, the complete safe set is $[0,1]$;
\item if $D\le\F_p^*$ has order $512$, the rows
\[
 (k,a)=(256,351),(128,233),(64,143),(32,52)
\]
are safe at radii $161/512$, $279/512$, $369/512$, and $115/128$, respectively, and every point is strictly beyond the exact MDS Johnson radius.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\source{Official saturation and the four length-$512$ shortening/circuit certificates are in \cite{ChoShort26}.}

\begin{theorem}[First adjacent threshold over separating fields]\label{thm:separating}
Put $M=\binom n{k+1}=\binom n{n-k-1}$.  If
\[
 \abs\F>\max\left\{M,\binom M2\right\},
\]
then $B_C^{\MCA}(k+1)=M$.  For $b=\floor{\eps\abs\F}$, the first safe agreement is $k+1$ when $b\ge M$, and it is $k+2$ whenever
\[
 \binom n{k+2}\le b<M.
\]
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite[Exact first adjacent row over a separating field]{ChoGF26}.}

\begin{theorem}[Large scalar-extension capacity regime]\label{thm:extension-capacity}
Let $C_n=\RS_{\F_n}(D_n,k_n)$ with $D_n\subseteq\B_n\subseteq\F_n$, $\abs{D_n}=n$, and $k_n/n\to\rho$.  If
\[
 \frac{a_n}{n}\to\alpha,
 \qquad (a_n-k_n-1)\log\abs{\B_n}=o(n),
\]
and
\[
 \frac1n\log\abs{\F_n}\to\phi,
 \qquad \frac1n\log(\abs{\F_n}-n)\to\phi,
\]
then $\alpha=\rho$ and
\[
 \frac1n\log B_{C_n}^{\MCA}(a_n)=\min\{h(\rho),\phi\}+o(1).
\]
For targets $\eps_n=e^{-\lambda n+o(n)}$ with $\lambda<\phi-h(\rho)$, the first permitted agreement $k_n+1$ is safe and the threshold is $1-\rho+o(1)$.
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite[Large scalar-extension exponent]{ChoGF26}.}

\begin{theorem}[Generalized exact high-agreement ledger]\label{thm:high-ledger}
Let $C=\RS_{\F}(D,k)$, put $R=n-k$, and write $r=n-a$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=*]
\item If $r\le\floor{R/3}$, then the finite affine support-wise MCA numerator, no-loss CA numerator, and projective-slope support-wise numerator all equal $r+1$.
\item For every interleaving arity $\mu\ge1$, if $r\le\floor{R/2}$, then the common-support list size satisfies $\Lambda_\mu(C,a)=1$.
\item For the finite-parameter degree-$d$ power curve
\[
 f_0+\gamma f_1+\cdots+\gamma^df_d,
\]
if $r\le\floor{R/(d+2)}$, then the support-wise curve-MCA and no-loss curve-CA numerators both equal $\min\{\abs\F,d(r+1)\}$.
\item If a coding ledger uses curve or line degrees $d_1,\ldots,d_s$ and $\ell$ interleaved-list terms over one denominator $Q$, then in the common exact range
\[
 N_{\mathrm{coding}}(r)=\ell+\sum_{i=1}^s d_i(r+1).
\]
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite{HighLedger26}.  The special $\mathbb F_{17^{32}}$ adjacent line/list ledger is in \cite{F17Threshold26}.}

\begin{remark}[Line-decoding terminology]\label{rem:line}
The support-wise numerator $LD_{\mathrm{sw}}$ satisfies
\[
 e_{\MCA}(C,\delta)=\frac{LD_{\mathrm{sw}}(C,\ceil{(1-\delta)n})}{\abs\F}.
\]
This is not the stronger Goyal--Guruswami notion of $(\delta,A,B)$ line-decodability.  The latter implies MCA through Theorem~4.21 of \cite{ABF26}, whereas ordinary close-point line decoding can be strictly larger than the support-wise numerator.  The exact bridge, residue-line reformulation, and separation example are in \cite{LineBridge26}.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[List consequences of CA/MCA bounds]\label{cor:abf}
Let $C=\RS_{\F}(D,k)$ and $C^+=\RS_{\F}(D,k+1)$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=*]
\item Under the hypotheses of Theorem~5.2 of \cite{ABF26}, sufficiently small CA error implies $\abs{\Lambda(C,\delta)}<\abs\F$.
\item Under the hypotheses of Theorem~5.3 of \cite{ABF26}, a CA error bound for $C$ gives
\[
 \abs{\Lambda(C^+,\delta)}
 \le\ceil{\frac{\abs\F}{1-\eta}\,e_{\CA}(C,\delta)}.
\]
\item In the quadratic staircase range, substituting the exact MCA numerator $r+1$ gives the explicit integer consequences stated in \cite[Line decoding and list-decoding consequences]{ChoThresholds26}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{corollary}
\source{Theorems~5.2 and~5.3 of \cite{ABF26}; the exact deep-point form of item (ii) is also in \cite{ChoCap26}.}

\section{Universal unsafe caps and exact failure edges}

\begin{theorem}[Universal field-size cap]\label{thm:cap}
Let $\rho\in\{1/2,1/4,1/8,1/16\}$, let $\abs\F<2^{256}$, let $D\subseteq\B^*$ be a multiplicative coset of order $n$, and let $k=\rho n\le2^{40}$.  Assume $1024\mid n$ for $\rho\ge1/8$ and $2048\mid n$ for $\rho=1/16$.  At target $2^{-128}$,
\[
 \delta_C^*\le1-\rho-2^{-9}
 \quad(\rho=1/2,1/4,1/8),
\]
and
\[
 \delta_C^*\le1-\rho-2^{-10}
 \quad(\rho=1/16).
\]
Throughout the corresponding terminal interval the MCA error is greater than $2^{-86}$; if $\abs\F\ge2n$, it is greater than $2^{-42}$.
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite[Universal field-size cap]{ChoCap26}.}

\begin{corollary}[Map-smooth and circle transport]\label{cor:transport}
The same cap applies, with the multiplicity correction stated in \cite{ChoCap26}, to map-smooth images whose fibers have uniformly bounded size.  In particular, it applies to the normalized circle/Chebyshev presentation after accounting for the two-to-one torus map and the exceptional branch points.
\end{corollary}
\source{\cite[Map-smooth and circle cap theorems]{ChoCap26}.}

\section{Primary structural results at the deployed frontier}

The next statements are unconditional theorems used by the current finite completion architecture.  They are not, individually or collectively, advertised here as a completed adjacent-row proof.

\begin{theorem}[Saturation and split-pencil payments]\label{thm:saturation-pencil}
Let $U$ be a received word and let $s_c(U)$ be its agreement count with $c\in C$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)},leftmargin=*]
\item The exact support-locator saturation count at agreement $m$ is
\[
 \operatorname{Cen}(U;m)=\sum_{c\in C}\binom{s_c(U)}m.
\]
Thus raw support multiplicity must not be identified with codeword or slope multiplicity.
\item For a projective polynomial pencil $sA+tB$, with common domain-root locator of degree $g$, if every counted member has at least $h$ moving roots outside the common root set, then the number of counted projective parameters is at most
\[
 \floor{\frac{n-g}{h}}.
\]
\item After the tangent, common-support, quotient, extension, degree-drop, and common-GCD branches are removed, every primitive one-parameter split-pencil chart at the active KoalaBear or Mersenne MCA row contributes at most two finite bad slopes.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\source{\cite[Saturation identity and moving-root split-pencil theorem]{ChoGF26}.}

\begin{theorem}[Base-field coordinate-span confinement]\label{thm:coordinate-span}
Let $\B=\F_p\subseteq\F$, let $D\subseteq\B$, and let $y_0,y_1$ be the syndromes of a received line.  Every nonzero finite bad slope satisfies
\[
 \gamma\in L_{\B}(y_0)L_{\B}(y_1)^{-1}.
\]
If $s=\dim_{\B}L_{\B}(y_0)$ and $u=\dim_{\B}L_{\B}(y_1)$, then the affine bad set has size at most
\[
 1+\frac{(p^s-1)(p^u-1)}{p-1},
\]
and the projective bad set has size at most the same quantity plus one.  A dependent syndrome pair has one bad projective parameter, and a syndrome plane containing two rank-one projective points has projective bad set of size at most $p+1$.
\end{theorem}
\source{Danny, \cite{DannyCoordinateSpan26}.  The exact rank-$(1,2)$ radial occupancy problem remains open.}

\begin{theorem}[Pole-tolerant scalar-locator localization]\label{thm:pole}
For a coherent scalar-locator certificate
\[
 Qh_i+(c_0+c_1\gamma_i)\Lambda_i=A+\gamma_iB,
\]
define
\[
 G_Q=\{x\in D:A(x)=Q(x)r_0(x),\ B(x)=Q(x)r_1(x)\},
 \qquad g=\abs{G_Q}.
\]
Every coordinate outside $G_Q$ lies in at most one selected support.  Consequently,
\[
 \abs I\le\floor{\frac{n-g}{m-g}}\le n-m+1
 \quad(g<m),
\]
and
\[
 \abs I\le n-g+B^{\MCA}_{\F,G_Q,k,\Gamma}(m)
 \quad(g\ge m).
\]
For exact monic locators, after deleting at most one zero-scalar slope, every common denominator pole is a common support point and can be divided from $Q,A,B$, and all live locators while preserving the scalar-locator identity and degree profile.
\end{theorem}
\source{Danny, \cite{DannyPole26}.  This is a local algebraic theorem; a chronology-correct first-match compiler is still required.}

\begin{theorem}[$D_{128}$ Chebyshev package]\label{thm:d128}

\section{Conclusion}

The repository now contains several complete solutions of genuine Proximity Prize subfamilies, including four maximal-dimension smooth prime-field thresholds and the exact $\mathbb F_{17^{32}}$ smooth row.  It also contains the strongest currently audited large-field safe edges at rates $1/4$, $1/8$, and $1/16$, universal near-capacity failure caps, exact deployed unsafe floors, and a row-independent line/curve/list coding ledger.  The KoalaBear and Mersenne programmes have advanced from undifferentiated residuals to banked atoms, precise algebraic localizations, finite rank closures, exact counterexample floors, and a forced critical-core dichotomy.  The adjacent deployed safe rows are still open and are not claimed here.  Every result in this paper is stated without reproducing its proof and points to the paper, note, or certificate packet where the proof and exact arithmetic are available.
